About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the San Diego Supercomputer Center (SDSC), where my research spans three interconnected areas: scientific workflows, AI for Science, and AI for the Humanities.

My work in scientific workflows focuses on understanding how workflow structure impacts performance in high-performance computing (HPC) environments. I develop algorithms, benchmarks, and data-driven techniques to optimize workflow execution. As part of the National Data Platform (NDP) effort, I explore how to better support workflows at scale—enhancing their efficiency, scalability, and reproducibility across diverse computational platforms.

As part of the Societal Computing and Innovation Lab (SCIL) at SDSC, I contribute to AI for Science by advancing the NDP to make AI services more accessible and impactful. This includes designing infrastructure and methodologies that lower barriers for scientists to use AI in their research.

I am also actively engaged in extending NDP to support AI for the Humanities. I’m particularly interested in applying AI to domains such as language revitalization, digital art, and historical archives, where technology can amplify cultural preservation and community storytelling.

Driven by deep curiosity and a strong sense of community, I strive to bridge theory and practice in all my work, creating research that is both innovative and inclusive.
